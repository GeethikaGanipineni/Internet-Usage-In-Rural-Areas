About Jagadevipeta Village

A traditional rural community with rich culture and growing aspirations for digital connectivity

🏠 Village Overview

Jagadevipeta is a rural village located in Indukurupeta Mandal, SPSR Nellore District, Andhra Pradesh. The community primarily depends on agriculture and fishing. Our CSP 2026 survey covered 20 residents to study the current state of internet adoption in the village.

🕐 Historical Profile

Established during the Kakatiya dynasty era, the village is named after its patron deity "Jagadevi." Annual festivals honour this heritage and strengthen community bonds across generations.

🎓 Community Diversity

Multiple communities — Hindu, Muslim, and Christian families — live harmoniously, each contributing uniquely to the village's cultural fabric. Decisions are made through democratic panchayat assemblies.

🌟 Traditions & Development

Sankranti, Diwali, and Eid are celebrated together. Recent years have brought improved roads, stable electricity, and partial mobile network coverage. A government school and primary health centre serve residents.

Barriers

Challenges Faced

Key obstacles to digital adoption identified during our study

🔻
Poor Network Coverage

Large areas of the village experience weak or zero signal. Remote farm zones have virtually no connectivity.

👤
Digital Literacy Gap

Elders and first-time users lack basic smartphone skills. English-only interfaces create additional language barriers.

💰
Affordability Issues

Upfront smartphone costs and monthly data plans remain prohibitive for low-income families.

🚫
Lack of Awareness

Many villagers are unaware of practical internet benefits. Fear of scams discourages adoption even among those who can afford it.

Recommendations

Suggestions

Actionable steps for government, NGOs, and communities

1
Improve Infrastructure

Expand BharatNet fiber to all gram panchayats. Incentivize telecom companies to install towers in underserved zones.

2
Digital Literacy Programs

Establish village-level digital literacy centers with Telugu-language training focused on UPI, health apps, and government portals.

3
Affordable Internet Plans

Introduce special rural subsidy plans and community data bundles. Expand PM-WANI WiFi hotspots in common areas.

4
Government Support

Simplify e-governance portals with local language support. Provide subsidized smartphones through existing welfare schemes.

5
Community Awareness Campaigns

Engage youth as Digital Champions to mentor elders. Conduct workshops in schools, temples, and community halls. Use local radio and TV for broader reach.

Findings & Conclusion

Project Findings & Final Conclusion

Learning outcomes, key observations, and our final takeaways

📊
Survey Summary

20 people surveyed in Jagadevipeta Village, Indukurupeta Mandal, SPSR Nellore District. 13 use the internet; 7 do not.

🌎
Internet Adoption

65% of surveyed residents (13 of 20) actively use the internet for education, agriculture, communication, and information.

🔍
Key Observations

Students learn online; farmers check weather and crop info; youth use social media. Elderly and less-aware residents form the 35% non-users.

🎓
Knowledge Gained

More awareness programs and digital literacy drives are needed to help the remaining 35% adopt internet services confidently.

🏁 Final Conclusion

Our CSP 2026 survey of 20 residents in Jagadevipeta Village, Indukurupeta Mandal, SPSR Nellore District revealed that the majority — 65% (13 of 20 people) — already use internet services. Internet usage is actively improving education, communication, agriculture, and access to government information in the village. Students use it for online learning, farmers use it for weather and crop guidance, and youth rely on it for social media and communication. However, the remaining 35% (7 of 20 people) are not yet using the internet, primarily due to lack of awareness and digital literacy. More targeted awareness programs and digital literacy drives are needed to help these residents confidently adopt and benefit from internet technology.

"Digital connectivity is not a luxury for rural India — it is the bridge to a better future."
